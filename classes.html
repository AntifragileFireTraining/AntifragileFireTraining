Classes

Department Training

Lecture/Hands-on Training
2-4 Days

Over the past six years I have been fortunate enough to work with, and learn from multiple departments across the US and Canada. Whether working with the training division or with the entire department, the precept is always the same...collaborate with the department to become as knowledgeable and skillful as we can together. This typically takes a lot of work on the front-end before class and continues on the back-end well after class is over, but is also my favorite way to teach.

By the end of our time together students will have a much better conceptual understanding of the fireground. Students will also have a much better practical understanding of how our actions will influence the fire, searchable space, and most importantly, THEM. Students will leave with enhanced functional insight with regards to coordinated operations, controlling flow path(s), size-up, path of removal, and the importance of increasing our tempo on the fire/training ground.

To give the students as much usable, actionable, tactical information regarding search, rescue, fire dynamics, and coordination as possible, we will make sure we spend our time focusing on:

- Solidifying WHY search is so important.
- Enhancing our search technique and tempo.
- Enhancing communication within the fire building on the on the fireground.
- Improving decision-making on the fireground.
- To better deal with the inevitable friction on the fireground.
- Experimental data on what enhances survivability inside a structure fire.
- Practical techniques to increase survivability for victims.
- Ultimately, increasing the number of rescues the fie service makes.

FRS & FSRI: Facts & Physics of the Fireground

Lecture
2-4 Hours

This class will analyze data from over 5,000 actual fireground rescues and the ongoing fire dynamics research being done by UL FSRI, all while focusing on what is best for those trapped inside a burning building. This is the first time that the American fire service has tactical, actionable information on search and rescue. The overall goal of this class is to use both the experiential and the experimental evidence to help enhance our efficacy and efficiency on our future firegrounds…for THEM. Specifically, we will discuss search size-up, Firefighter Rescue Survey statistics, UL FSRI’s groundbreaking Search Study, how to coordinate search with attack and ventilation, decision-making on the fireground, and best practices for our search and rescue operations based on both, science AND the street.

Church of Search

Lecture
2-6 Hours

Like everything else in the fire service, the devil is in the details; and when it comes to search, so is a successful rescue. We all know that “as the first line goes, so goes the fire”, although oftentimes more importantly, ‘as the primary goes, so goes the victim(s).’ This class focuses on practical ways that we can search smarter, refine rescue, and coordinate and communicate more clearly during the fog of the fireground. Using data as our guide, we will start the conversation upstream of the incident, continue through the fireground operation, ven and then downstream of the incident with patient care and treatment. The basic question we hope to answer with this discussion is simple...If there is a fire in my loved one's house a year from today, what should we be doing over the next year, during the incident, and after we remove them from the building?

Grab Lab

Hands-on Training
1-2 Days

LIFE SAVING is the focus. This class will use data from thousands of actual fireground rescues and hundreds of experiments to put us in the best position possible to locate viable victims and remove them via the best route possible. We will practice rescue and search skills that have demonstrated, time and again, fireground success. We will spend the first part of class highlighting The WHY, HOW, and WHAT is necessary to save lives: mindset, size-up, movement, communication, coordination, and decision making. Then we will break into coaching stations covering drags (on-grade, up and down stairs, out windows, and down ladders), VES, oriented/split search, engine-based search, and decision making. We will then spend the remainder of class reinforcing the importance of proper tempo and decision-making on the fireground using realistic, guided drills, with some of the best instructors in the fire service.This is not hyperbole, the instructors that help with this class are incredibly skilled and knowledgeable and have included: Brian Abbott, Jeff Bryant, Matt Burke, Eddie Crombie, Sean Duffy, Shawn Kerr, Patch Lewis, Jon Lockwood, Justin Lorenzen, Jeff Luse, Seth Majors, Dustin Martinez, Larry McCormack, Taylor Quinnell, Jeff Rothmeier, Grant Schwalbe, Ben Shultz, Scott Vadakin, Joe Walsh, and Justin Yanosick.
